Early Recovery Tips

The first few weeks of recovery are crucial for building a strong foundation. Here are essential strategies to help you navigate this critical period:

Focus on the Basics

  • • Establish a consistent sleep schedule
  • • Eat regular, nutritious meals
  • • Stay hydrated throughout the day
  • • Take prescribed medications as directed

Build Your Support Network

  • • Attend recovery meetings regularly
  • • Find a sponsor or mentor
  • • Connect with sober peers
  • • Maintain contact with treatment providers

Preparing for Sober Living

Transitioning to sober living is a significant step. Proper preparation ensures a smooth adjustment and increases your chances of success.

What to Bring

Personal Items

  • • Clothing for 1-2 weeks
  • • Personal hygiene items
  • • Bedding and pillow
  • • Important documents

Recovery Tools

  • • Recovery books/workbooks
  • • Journal for reflection
  • • Meeting schedule
  • • Emergency contact list

Prohibited Items

  • • Alcohol or drugs
  • • Weapons of any kind
  • • Prescription drugs not prescribed to you
  • • Items that violate house rules

Setting Recovery Goals

Goal setting provides direction and motivation in recovery. Use the SMART criteria to create achievable objectives.

SMART Goals Framework

  • S Specific: Clear and well-defined
  • M Measurable: Track progress
  • A Achievable: Realistic expectations
  • R Relevant: Meaningful to you
  • T Time-bound: Set deadlines

Goal Categories

  • • Sobriety milestones (30, 60, 90 days)
  • • Employment and career objectives
  • • Financial stability targets
  • • Relationship improvements
  • • Health and wellness goals
  • • Educational pursuits

Building Healthy Routines

Structured daily routines provide stability and purpose in recovery. They help replace chaotic patterns with positive habits.

Sample Daily Schedule

Morning (6:00 AM - 12:00 PM)

  • 6:00 AM - Wake up, personal hygiene
  • 6:30 AM - Morning meditation/reflection
  • 7:00 AM - Breakfast and house duties
  • 8:00 AM - Work/job search/education
  • 12:00 PM - Lunch break

Evening (5:00 PM - 10:00 PM)

  • 5:00 PM - Return home, check-in
  • 6:00 PM - Dinner and cleanup
  • 7:00 PM - Recovery meeting/group activity
  • 8:30 PM - Personal time/study
  • 10:00 PM - Quiet hours begin

Stress Management

Learning to manage stress without substances is crucial for long-term recovery. Develop healthy coping strategies that work for you.

Immediate Techniques

  • • Deep breathing exercises
  • • Progressive muscle relaxation
  • • Call your sponsor
  • • Take a walk
  • • Listen to calming music

Daily Practices

  • • Regular exercise routine
  • • Mindfulness meditation
  • • Journaling
  • • Adequate sleep
  • • Healthy nutrition

Long-term Strategies

  • • Therapy or counseling
  • • Stress management classes
  • • Hobby development
  • • Social support building
  • • Time management skills

Developing Healthy Habits

Recovery is about replacing destructive patterns with positive ones. Focus on building habits that support your physical, mental, and emotional well-being.

Physical Health

  • ✓ Regular exercise (30 min daily)
  • ✓ Balanced nutrition and meal planning
  • ✓ 7-8 hours of quality sleep
  • ✓ Regular medical check-ups

Mental/Emotional Health

  • ✓ Daily gratitude practice
  • ✓ Mindfulness or meditation
  • ✓ Regular therapy sessions
  • ✓ Creative expression activities

Conflict Resolution

Living in close quarters with others can create conflicts. Learning healthy resolution skills is essential for maintaining harmony and personal growth.

The CALM Approach

C

Cool Down

Take time to manage emotions

A

Acknowledge

Recognize all perspectives

L

Listen

Practice active listening

M

Mediate

Find mutually beneficial solutions

Do's

  • • Use "I" statements
  • • Stay focused on the issue
  • • Listen to understand
  • • Seek house manager help if needed

Don'ts

  • • Don't attack the person
  • • Don't bring up past issues
  • • Don't involve other residents
  • • Don't let emotions escalate

Peer Support Groups

Connecting with others who understand your journey is invaluable. Peer support provides encouragement, accountability, and shared wisdom.

12-Step Programs

  • • Alcoholics Anonymous (AA): For alcohol addiction recovery
  • • Narcotics Anonymous (NA): For drug addiction recovery
  • • Cocaine Anonymous (CA): Specific to cocaine addiction
  • • Crystal Meth Anonymous (CMA): For meth addiction

Alternative Support Groups

  • • SMART Recovery: Science-based approach
  • • Refuge Recovery: Buddhist-inspired program
  • • LifeRing: Secular recovery support
  • • Women/Men for Sobriety: Gender-specific groups

Building Healthy Relationships

Recovery involves learning to form and maintain healthy relationships. This includes setting boundaries, communicating effectively, and choosing supportive people.

Healthy Boundaries

  • • Say no to enabling behaviors
  • • Limit contact with toxic people
  • • Protect your recovery time
  • • Communicate your needs clearly

Communication Skills

  • • Practice active listening
  • • Express feelings honestly
  • • Ask for what you need
  • • Resolve conflicts peacefully

Relationship Types

  • • Sponsor/mentee relationships
  • • Sober friendships
  • • Family reconciliation
  • • Professional relationships

Giving Back to Community

Service to others is a cornerstone of recovery. Giving back provides purpose, builds self-esteem, and strengthens your commitment to sobriety.

Ways to Give Back

Within Recovery Community

  • • Sponsor newcomers in your program
  • • Help set up meeting rooms
  • • Share your story at meetings
  • • Volunteer at recovery events
  • • Support house activities

In the Broader Community

  • • Volunteer at local charities
  • • Mentor youth programs
  • • Participate in community cleanup
  • • Help at food banks
  • • Support animal shelters

Accountability Partners

Accountability partnerships provide mutual support, honest feedback, and motivation to stay committed to recovery goals.

Finding the Right Partner

  • ✓ Similar recovery timeline and goals
  • ✓ Commitment to honesty and growth
  • ✓ Available for regular check-ins
  • ✓ Positive, supportive attitude

Regular Activities

  • • Weekly progress check-ins
  • • Goal setting and review sessions
  • • Attend meetings together
  • • Celebrate milestones and achievements
